## The Core Philosophy: Symbiotic Growth

### Product & Distribution as Conjoined Twins

Greg's central thesis: **Your product's value proposition and distribution channel should be inseparable.**

Traditional approach:
1. Build product
2. Figure out distribution later
3. Pay for ads to get users

**Symbiotic approach:**
1. Distribution is embedded in the product itself
2. Users become your distribution channel
3. Product spreads organically

### The 5 Types of Symbiotic Products

| Type | Mechanism | Examples |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Template-based** | Users create content that promotes the tool | Notion templates, Canva designs, Figma files |
| **Watermark/ attribution** | Every use includes brand exposure | CapCut videos, Loom recordings, Typeform surveys |
| **Network effects** | Product gets better as more people use it | Figma, Linear, Discord |
| **Content output** | Product generates shareable artifacts | Ecosia search counter, Audible clips, Spotify Wrapped |
| **Community-embedded** | Product lives inside existing communities | Reddit bots, Discord apps, Slack integrations |

### How to Design for Symbiotic Growth

Ask these questions during product design:

1. **"What will the TikTok look like?"**
   - Not "What will the press release say?"
   - Visual, shareable moments built into the product

2. **"How do users become marketers?"**
   - Every user should have a reason to invite others
   - Incentives aligned with value delivery

3. **"What is the viral loop?"**
   - Map the path from one user to the next
   - Remove friction at every step

## Framework 1: Tweet-Sized Landing Pages (TSLPs)

### The Problem with Traditional Landing Pages

- Too much copy (users don't read)
- Confusing CTAs
- Generic stock photos
- Feels like a sales pitch

### The TSLP Formula

**280 characters maximum** of copy on your landing page.

Structure:
```
[Hook - 5-7 words]

[One sentence describing the transformation]

[Clear CTA for free thing]
```

### Examples

**Notion (early):**
```
One workspace.

All your notes, tasks, and wikis. Finally together.

Get Notion free →
```

**Linear (early):**
```
The issue tracker you'll enjoy using.

Built for speed. Designed for clarity. Loved by teams.

Try Linear free →
```

### Why TSLPs Convert 300% Better

1. **Mystery creates urgency** — Users need to know what's on the other side
2. **Less cognitive load** — Decision happens in seconds
3. **Feels exclusive** — Like being let in on a secret
4. **Mobile-optimized** — Most traffic is mobile; TSLPs are perfect for it

### TSLP Anti-Patterns

❌ **Don't:** Long feature lists  
✅ **Do:** One clear transformation

❌ **Don't:** Corporate stock photos  
✅ **Do:** Weird, playful, or mysterious imagery

❌ **Don't:** Multiple CTAs  
✅ **Do:** One clear action

❌ **Don't:** Explain everything  
✅ **Do:** Create curiosity gap

## Framework 2: 30-Day Launch Model

### The Compressed Timeline

Greg's framework for going from idea to launched product in 30 days.

### Week 1: Birth of Idea

**Days 1-2: Define the problem**
- What specific pain point are you solving?
- Who feels this pain most acutely?
- How do they solve it today (workarounds)?

**Days 3-4: Define the solution**
- What's your "quantum of utility"?
- What's the minimum viable version?
- Can you describe it in one tweet?

**Days 5-7: Define GTM strategy**
- Which symbiotic growth type fits?
- Where does your audience hang out?
- What's your distribution hook?

### Week 2: Digital Real Estate

**Days 8-9: Secure domain & socials**
- Buy domain immediately (don't overthink)
- Claim Twitter/X, TikTok, Instagram handles
- Set up simple landing page (Carrd, Webflow)

**Days 10-12: Design sprint**
- Logo and basic brand (use AI tools)
- Essential assets only
- "Dispatch" mindset — ship fast

**Days 13-14: Build in public**
- Start posting about the journey
- Share behind-the-scenes
- Build anticipation

### Week 3: Test Demand

**Days 15-18: Landing page experiments**
- $1,000 test budget (or time equivalent)
- A/B test messaging
- Measure sign-up rates

**Days 19-21: Validate with real users**
- Get 10 people to try it
- Watch them use it (don't explain)
- Note where they get stuck

### Week 4: Launch

**Days 22-25: Final polish**
- Fix critical bugs only
- Optimize onboarding
- Prepare launch assets

**Days 26-28: Soft launch**
- Friends and family
- Small community (Discord, subreddit)
- Gather testimonials

**Days 29-30: Public launch**
- Product Hunt
- Hacker News
- Twitter/X announcement

### The "Apple and Orange" Principle

> "Don't work hard to make an 'orange' when your market wants an 'apple.'"

- Quick, messy steps forward > waiting for perfection
- Let community buzz guide pivots
- Launch something bad, quickly

## Framework 3: Products That Spread Like Wildfire

### The Viral Product Checklist

Products that spread organically have these traits:

| Trait | Why It Works | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **Novel** | Feels new and different | BeReal, Locket |
| **Fun** | Using it is enjoyable | Duolingo, TikTok |
| **Simple** | No learning curve | Instagram, Snapchat |
| **Unnecessary** | Not mission-critical, but desirable | Cameo, NGL |
| **Shareable** | Creates content worth sharing | Letterboxd, Goodreads |

### The TikTok Test

Before building, ask: **"What will the TikTok look like?"**

- Can someone demo your product in 15 seconds?
- Is the value proposition immediately obvious?
- Does it make the viewer want to try it?

If you can't imagine the TikTok, reconsider the product.

### Undetectable Copywriting

Write copy that feels:
- **Human**, not corporate
- **Playful**, not serious
- **Personal**, not salesy
- **Curious**, not explanatory

**Example:**

❌ "Our platform leverages AI to optimize workflows"  
✅ "The to-do list that actually gets done"

❌ "Schedule a demo with our sales team"  
✅ "Try it free →"

## Framework 4: Faceless Brands

### Build Around Transformations, Not People

**Faceless brands** dominate niches without relying on a personal founder brand.

### The Formula

1. **Claim a category** — Own a specific transformation
2. **Sub-niche first** — Dominate a small market, then expand
3. **Value ladder** — Free → Low-cost → Premium
4. **Consistent output** — Regular content without burnout

### Examples

| Brand | Category | Transformation |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| **Duolingo** | Language learning | Zero to conversational |
| **Morning Brew** | Business news | Clueless to informed |
| **The Points Guy** | Travel rewards | Paying full price to flying free |
| **StripMallGuy** | Real estate investing | W-2 to property owner |

### Why Faceless Brands Work

- **Scalable** — Not tied to founder's time
- **Sellable** — Can exit without losing audience
- **Trust through consistency** — Clear intention, no personal drama
- **Easier to hire** — Team can contribute without founder's voice

## Launch Tactics by Platform

### Product Hunt

**Pre-launch:**
- Build hunter relationships 2-4 weeks ahead
- Prepare assets: GIFs, screenshots, one-liner
- Line up 20+ supporters for launch day

**Launch day:**
- Post at 12:01 AM PT
- Respond to every comment within 5 minutes
- Keep the momentum going all day

**Post-launch:**
- Thank supporters publicly
- Convert hunters to users
- Analyze what worked

### Hacker News

**What works:**
- Technical depth and transparency
- "Show HN" posts with working demos
- Founder stories and lessons learned

**What doesn't:**
- Marketing speak
- Press releases
- Generic product announcements

### Twitter/X

**Build in public:**
- Share the journey, not just the destination
- Post daily about progress
- Engage with community authentically

**Launch thread structure:**
1. Hook (problem or surprising fact)
2. Story (why you built it)
3. Demo (show, don't tell)
4. CTA (clear next step)
5. Engagement (reply to everyone)

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### 1. The Perfection Trap

❌ Waiting for perfect product  
✅ Launching with quantum of utility

> "If you're not embarrassed by your first version, you launched too late."

### 2. The Big Bang Launch

❌ One massive launch event  
✅ Continuous shipping and iteration

### 3. The Feature Dump

❌ Listing every feature  
✅ Leading with transformation

### 4. The Copycat

❌ Building what competitors have  
✅ Solving a real problem your way

### 5. The Paid Addiction

❌ Relying on Facebook/Google ads  
✅ Building organic distribution

### 6. The Lone Wolf

❌ Building in secret  
✅ Building in public with community
