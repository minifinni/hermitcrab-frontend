## Analysis & Recommendations

### Top 3 Picks to Start With

1. **Andrej Karpathy** (AI/LLM Engineering)
   - **Why:** The definitive voice in LLM education right now. His content is timeless (first-principles), has massive reach (500K+), and is exactly what users want when they ask AI about "how LLMs work." His "Zero to Hero" neural networks series and recent LLM deep-dives are pure gold.
   - **Content Volume:** ~30 videos, but each is 1-2 hours of dense content
   - **Uniqueness:** Former Tesla AI + OpenAI insider teaching from first principles

2. **ByteByteGo / Alex Xu** (System Design)
   - **Why:** System design is a core competency gap for many developers. Alex Xu has literally written the book (twice). His content is structured, comprehensive, and authoritative. An AI that can answer "how to design this system" with his methodology would be incredibly valuable.
   - **Content Volume:** 100+ videos, 2 books, newsletter
   - **Uniqueness:** Ex-Meta staff engineer with proven framework for system design interviews AND real-world architecture

3. **Theo Browne (t3dotgg)** (Web Dev/Indie Hacking)
   - **Why:** Bridges the gap between "big tech engineering" and "indie founder shipping." His takes on Next.js, TypeScript, and modern web architecture are influential. He has strong, sometimes controversial opinions that would give an AI assistant personality.
   - **Content Volume:** 500+ videos, daily streams
   - **Uniqueness:** Former Twitch eng turned successful indie founder, very current on modern web stack

### Honorable Mention: **Pieter Levels**
- If hermitcrab wants a purely "indie hacker" skill, Pieter is unmatched. He doesn't have the YouTube volume of others, but his methodology ("just ship," "boring technology," "build in public") is a complete philosophy. Would need to extract from Twitter, blog, and limited video content.

### Gaps in the Ecosystem

1. **DevOps/Infrastructure at Depth**
   - Most DevOps content is tutorial-heavy. Missing: A true "infrastructure architect" voice with 50+ deep videos on Kubernetes internals, cloud-native patterns, and platform engineering. Hussein's content is closest but not quite system-design-level depth.
   - **Gap status:** Partial coverage (Fireship for breadth, Hussein for depth, but missing the "ByteByteGo of DevOps")

2. **Mobile Development (iOS/Android)**
   - Very few creators with both depth AND volume. Most are tutorial-focused.
   - **Gap status:** Significant gap

3. **Security Engineering (Not Pentesting)**
   - Lots of CTF/pentesting content (John Hammond, etc.), but missing: "How to build secure systems" from an application security architect perspective. Web security for developers is underserved.
   - **Gap status:** Moderate gap

4. **Data Engineering (Not ML)**
   - Greg Kamradt covers some, but missing a true "modern data stack" authority with depth on dbt, data modeling, ETL patterns at scale.
   - **Gap status:** Moderate gap

5. **Product Management (Video Content)**
   - Lenny is newsletter/podcast-first. Missing a strong PM voice on YouTube with depth on discovery, roadmapping, stakeholder management.
   - **Gap status:** Partial coverage

6. **UX/Design for Developers**
   - Maggie Appleton is great but niche. Missing a comprehensive "design for developers" voice.
   - **Gap status:** Moderate gap

## Extraction Strategy Notes

### High-Priority Extraction Targets
1. **Andrej Karpathy** — Start with "Neural Networks: Zero to Hero" and "Intro to LLMs"
2. **ByteByteGo** — Focus on system design series, extract the repeatable patterns
3. **Theo Browne** — Extract architecture opinions and "why X not Y" content
4. **Sebastian Lague** — "Coding Adventures" series has unique methodology
5. **Simon Willison** — Blog + LLM tool demos, very extractable

### Content Formats Worth Targeting
- Long-form educational videos (1hr+) — highest knowledge density
- Conference talks — often contain distilled methodology
- Podcast interviews — good for capturing POV/opinions
- Newsletter/blog archives — structured, high signal

### Challenges
- Some best creators (Lenny, Arvid, Pieter) are text/audio-first — may need different extraction pipeline
- Livestream creators (Theo, Primeagen) have gold buried in long streams — needs clipping/transcription strategy
- Academic/research creators (Karpathy, Yannic) use heavy math — extraction needs to preserve intuition without getting lost in notation
